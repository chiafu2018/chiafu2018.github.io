# Course Content

The course teaches how to collaborate with others on project development, helping students become familiar with using Git and other essential skills. I personally think these skills are very important, but I couldn't find similar courses at NYCU. Perhaps my university focuses on teaching more specialized knowledge, considering these skills can be developed once students enter the workforce.

The followings are the five important topics which the course covered.

  1. Build System
  2. Unit Test
  3. Test Driven Development
  4. Development Tools
  5. Object Oriented Programming

# Teaching Method

The course was held in-person every two weeks, with lab sessions scheduled in the weeks without classes. The instructor speaks clearly, and the course content is straightforward, making it easy to understand.

# Exams & Homeworks

The course includes five assignments and one final exam, with assignments making up 50% of the total grade and the exam also accounting for 50%. Assignments are submitted through GitLab. The course isn't hared, making it an ideal course for exchange students who want to travel a lot.

# Experience

Since I didn't have any internship experience before this course, I initially struggled with using version control tools like Git. However, I learned many basic yet valuable concepts, which gave me a better understanding of the software development process. I'm also grateful to a senior in my group who helped me resolve several challenges along the way.
